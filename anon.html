Becoming Anonymous

Why would you ever need to become anonymous?

The United States of America collects data on all of their citizens using their online presence. The government monitors all of your social media posts, phone calls, text messages, google searches, every website you visit. By monitoring every move you make in the digital world, the government is able to identify you and figure out personal information about you. Although TikTok dances are super fun, it might be best to not post a video of you doing one. Here’s some other ways you can preserve your anonymity:

Zone 1: Easy Basic Privacy

  • Create a password manager with all the passwords you have created inside of it
  • Use two factor authentication
  • Use a default engine, only use trusted add-ons and extensions, and don't store cookies, history and cache (or clear them yourself)
  • Avoid free VPNs and proxies
  • Hide real information, don't talk to strangers, don't post in comments, don't post pictures of yourself and disable unnecessary permissions on social media
  • Avoid putting your phone number online, use google voice and hangouts for communication, and disable location settings, bluetooth, and settings that send usage data on your phone and computer

Zone 2: More Complex Privacy

  • Only use iMessage or Signal for text messaging and ProtonMail for emails
  • Use an iPhone as opposed to an Android because iPhones typically get more security updates
  • Periodically wipe your hard drive's free space using Exede cleaner and use drive encryption such as Bitlocker, for Windows, or File Locker, for Mac
  • Do not use an biometric, pin, voice-activated, fingerprint-activated, or default passwords
  • Avoid chrome-based browsers and disable flash and javascript

Zone 3: Drastic Privacy

  • Turn bluetooth and location off entirely
  • Password protect bios and storage drives
  • Don't use Google or any social media
  • Use a basic flip phone rather than a smart phone

Zone 4: Extreme Privacy

  • Don't use any phone
  • Spoof your MAC address using SMAP
  • Don't talk to strangers, don't give our personal information, change your speech patterns
  • Fill your email with mail bombers, upload random pictures of other people on your accounts, put random personal information that's different on each of your accounts, delete as many accounts as possible

To Test How Anonymous You Are:

  • Download the following PDF: https://techlore.tech/securityguide.pdf
  • On DuckDuckGo, type "What is my IP Address?" (your actual IP address should not show up)
  • Look yourself up on TinEye and make sure there isn’t too much information on you
  • Look yourself up on Archives
