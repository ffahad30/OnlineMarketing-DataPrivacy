Browser Fingerprinting

What does "Browser Fingerprinting" Mean?

Browser Fingerprinting is a way of identifying unique browsers and tracking online activities. Moreover, a device fingerprint is information collected about a remote computing device for the purpose of identification. They can be used to fully or partially identify individual users and or their devices; this happens even when cookies are turned off. Basically, when your phone or laptop connects to the internet the device will give specific data to the receiving server about the websites you visit. It is used to collect information about a lot: your browser type and version. Your operating system, active plugins, timezone, etc. Websites hold a large set of data of visitors so they can match against the fingerprints of their known users. The positive side of browser fingerprinting is that it can identify botnets. Botnets are a string of computers coordinated to perform a task and are often associated with malware. Analyzing botnets can help in investigations against fraudsters and can help with other suspicious activities.
