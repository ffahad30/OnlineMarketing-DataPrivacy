Surveillance Capitalism

What Does Surveillance Capitalism Mean?

Surveillance Capitalism works by providing free services for people, and while they use the services the providers of the services monitor the users behavior; sometimes this happens without their explicit consent. It takes human experience and it translates it into behavioural data. This data can be used to improve service, but it is also used to predict what the user will do in the future.

Its Origin

Surveillance Capitalism was invented around 2001 as a solution to the dotcom bust. Google, who originally was against advertising, started using all the data exhaust to boost ad revenue. This made advertising as important as search. It is the default model for capital accumulation in Silicon Valley, including startups and apps. In 2008 Sheryl Sandberg brought surveillance capitalism from Google to Facebook.
