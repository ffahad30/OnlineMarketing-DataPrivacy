How You Are Tracked Online

Trackers

A single site or app contains trackers which build a detailed profile of who you are and what you do online. Even when you have opted out of this process, companies might continue to build a profile on you.

Cookies

Cookies are tiny files placed on your computer by your browser when you visit websites. The two types of cookies are session cookies and persistent cookies. Session cookies allow you to easily move from one part of a website to another. They are stored only as long as your current session and should automatically be deleted when you leave the website. Persistent cookies are placed in your computer and stay put. They are used primarily by marking firms to track your browsing history. These cookies are intrusive to your privacy.

Browser Fingerprinting

When you visit a website, the javascript code is transferred to your browser, which executes this code. It can collect information about your installed extensions, browser types and names, time zones, screen resolution, adblock preferences, list of available fonts, computer hardware, etc. Javascript groups all your data together and calls it your browser fingerprint, which is then saved in a database.

VPN

A web server can read and understand your IP address, but a VPN can be used to disguise it. Although, a VPN only changes one of many points of data that form your browser fingerprint.

What Information Is Collected About You?

  • Gender
  • Age
  • Personal statutes
  • Address
  • Current location
  • Music
  • TV Shows
  • What we buy online and from where
This data creates individual profiles which monitor your behavior. These data profiles are sold to banks, landlords, and insurers to assess whether or not you will actually pay them efficiently. The marketing industry also uses your data to generate personalized ads for you.

How Can You Increase Your Privacy?

  • Clear browsing history
  • Clear cookies
  • Use privacy browsers
  • Use incognito mode
  • Pay attention to your apps' permission requests
  • Ask yourself if using these programs are worth your data being collected
