Facebook Dilemma

We already know that social media is the most influencing media there is. There are people whose jobs literally influence their followers to wear certain brands and believe certain ideals. Most of everything we do, especially as a teenager, can be boiled down to social media. To what extent does social media go?

What is With Facebook's Privacy Issues?:

Facebook’s main mission was to connect the world by sharing information.The company policy clearly shows what information gets collected and what is being sent out. Facebook clearly states that they have the right to collect different kinds of information from or about you once you accept the terms and agreement. Something that most people do not read despite the fact that social platforms encourage users to read the entire contract. The problem is that Facebook infringed its own privacy policy when the company unknowingly allowed an associated partner to sell the data for political purposes.

What Happens Now?

Mark Zuckerberg has tried to tell the world that Facebook is still committed to being a platform of sharing experiences with others. This popular and free service has to change their ways. They need to be transparent and tell their users explicitly what they are doing with their data. Facebook has said that they are going to be more thorough with notifying their users about changes to privacy policies. The multi-billion dollar firm is expecting users to understand the terms and conditions. In addition, users should understand that when they use Facebook and other social media services, that they are sharing their personal data to the world, and should take note that there is still a possibility for a breach in security.
